# Additional objective notes

## Category Target

How the story is structured or told: nonlinear timeline, unreliable
narrator, plot twist, anthology, single-location, two-hander, POV
mechanic, information control. Ask: "What storytelling device is used?"

## Coverage Decision

- Preferred: Keyword when a registry member directly names the device
  or structural form.
- Fallback: Semantic narrative_techniques when the craft pattern is
  real but untagged.
- Split: use Keyword for the covered device and Semantic only for the
  uncovered delivery mechanic or qualifier.

Do not convert general quality ("well written") into a device. Do not
invent craft details beyond the expression.

## Boundaries

- Pacing-as-experience or ending aftertaste -> Emotional / experiential.
- Physical format -> Format + visual-format specifics.
- Dialogue praise -> Dialogue craft acclaim.
- Writer credit -> Person credit.

## No-Fire

No-fire for vague storytelling praise, pure tone, ending mood, physical
format, or named writer.

## Body authoring — canonical labels verbatim, surface under-used sub-fields

When firing semantic `narrative_techniques`, two specific authoring rules:

**Canonical craft labels go in verbatim.** The ingest-side text uses
established technique names exactly as written in the field. Do not
paraphrase them:

- "non-linear timeline" — NOT "non-linear narrative" or "fragmented
  storyline".
- "unreliable narrator" — NOT "unreliable storytelling perspective".
- "Chekhov's gun" — NOT "early planted detail".
- "ticking clock deadline" — NOT "time-pressure escalation".
- "dramatic irony" — NOT "audience-knows-more positioning".
- "found-footage presentation" — NOT "POV-camera framing".
- "cliffhanger ending", "framed story", "story-within-a-story",
  "anthology segments", "fourth-wall breaks" — all canonical, all
  verbatim.

**Surface under-used sub-fields when the trait grounds them.** Most
rich queries touch 4–6 of the 9 sub-fields, not 1–2. Three sub-fields
are routinely under-utilized; populate them when applicable:

- `audience_character_perception` — for traits that name how the audience
  is positioned to read characters ("lovable rogue", "love-to-hate
  antagonist", "morally gray lead", "sympathetic monster"). This often
  gets missed when the trait names a character archetype.
- `conflict_stakes_design` — for traits about how stakes / pressure are
  built ("ticking clock deadline", "escalation ladder", "no-win
  dilemma", "Pyrrhic victory").
- `additional_narrative_devices` — for structural / framing / meta
  tricks ("cold open", "framed story", "story-within-a-story",
  "found-footage presentation", "epistolary format", "fourth-wall
  breaks", "genre deconstruction").
